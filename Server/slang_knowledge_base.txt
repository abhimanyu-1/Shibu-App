Adi Sakke: An expression of extreme excitement or approval, similar to 'Oh yeah!' or 'Awesome!'. Used when something goes very well.
Scene Contra: A situation that has gone wrong or become problematic. Synonymous with 'messy situation' or 'trouble'.
Poli: Means 'Great' or 'Superb'. Used to praise something or someone.
Pwoli: An emphasized version of 'Poli', meaning 'Absolutely smashing' or 'Terrific'.
Shokam: A sad or pathetic situation. Used when something disappointing happens.
Udayi: A fraud or a trickster. Someone who is not genuine.
Oola: A useless or incompetent person. Mildly derogatory.
Vibe: The mood or atmosphere. 'Nalla vibe' means good atmosphere.
Level: High class or standard. 'Thoora level' implies something is on another level (implies greatness).
Katta: Solid or strong support. 'Katta waiting' means waiting eagerly with full support.
Chunk: A very close friend, like a 'best buddy'.
Freak: Someone who is stylish or follows modern trends, often used for young people with funky hairstyles.
Kidu: Short for 'Kidilan', meaning excellent or fantastic.
Mass: Something very cool, heroic, or stylish, typically referring to movie scenes or heroic actions.
Kolam: A mess or disaster. 'Aake kolam aayi' means everything turned into a mess.
Theppu: Being ditched or cheated, especially in a romantic relationship.
Pani Kitti: Got into trouble or received a blow/punishment.
Moonji: Screwed up or failed miserably. A slightly vulgar term for being in a bad state.
Sambar: Ideally a dish, but can refer to a mix-up or a confused state of affairs.
Kilipoyi: Lost one's mind or became confused/dazed (literally 'bird flew away').
Dingolfi: A mix-up or a small trickery/confusion.
Thallu: Exaggeration or boasting. Someone who brags a lot is a 'Thallu Vandi'.
Java: Simple or easy? No, in this context it might mean old or technical. Wait, 'Java' in Kerala tech context might just mean 'Java'.
Maman: Literally uncle, but often refers to Police in a code language.
Sadhanam: Literally 'thing', but can refer to alcohol or a specific object depending on context.
Koppu: Nonsense or rubbish. Used to dismiss something.
Myre: A vulgar swear word, but often used colloquially among friends to mean 'guy' or just frustration. (Avoid using in professional context, but good for context).
Fund: Money or resources.
Set: Ready or okay. 'Scene set aanu' means everything is resolved or cool.
Scene: Example 'Scene aano?' means 'Is there a problem?'. 'Scene illa' means 'No problem'.
Dark: A bad or hopeless situation. 'Dark scene' means a grim situation.
Gum: Energy or spirit. 'Oru gum illa' means lacking energy.
Vattavada: A snack, but can be non-sense.
Chaya: Tea. The solution to all headaches.
Kadi: Snack or 'itching' (trouble). 'Veruthe kadi undakkathe' means don't create trouble for no reason.
Alamb: A messy or chaotic person/situation.
